INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Spatial pattern: Pearson r=+0.650 and Spearman rho=+0.695 agree and indicate a strong positive relationship between CPR_GRI and the official mosaic across the 2,141,932 co-valid pixels.
Absolute agreement is POOR (R^2=-23.83, i.e. worse than predicting the mosaic's mean everywhere) with a large systematic bias of +0.973. This is EXPECTED and traceable to a specific structural limitation of the power-only approximation, not a bug: CPR_GRI = 1 + 4*P_XP/(P_HH+P_VV) is bounded below by 1.0 by construction (the added term is never negative), whereas the official mosaic has median 0.331 -- i.e. predominantly OC-dominant, CPR<1 bare-regolith terrain. Reaching CPR<1 requires a positive Re(<S_HH S_VV*>) co-pol correlation term, which this approximation drops under the reflection-symmetry assumption (see cpr.py). The comparison itself is therefore direct empirical evidence that Re(<S_HH S_VV*>) is NOT negligible for this terrain, i.e. that reflection symmetry is a poor assumption here -- exactly the kind of information loss the task asked us to state explicitly rather than paper over.
Practical implication: CPR_GRI's ABSOLUTE values should not be compared against the official CPR>1 ice-candidate threshold. It may still be useful for RELATIVE / spatial-anomaly analysis (identifying pixels anomalous versus their local background) within a single GRI scene, but the exact SLI-based CPR pipeline remains the scientifically valid product for absolute CPR thresholding and should be preferred wherever SLI data exists.
Remaining scatter is also expected from: (a) different acquisition dates/geometry between this GRI scene and the 2025-06-30 mosaic, and (b) nearest-neighbour resampling error in the manual polar-stereographic georeferencing used for this comparison.
